About

ARCADIA Designworks is a small Industrial Design consulting company located in Portland, Maine. During the summer of 2016, I worked at ARCADIA in conjunction with Rollease Acmeda as a design and drafting consultant on an innovative barrel vault canopy project that is planned for commercial application and sale in 2017. In SolidWorks, I did all of the 3D modeling for the project in its beginning stages. I also got to learn from the lead designer about specific design methods, and communicate with the VP of design at Rollease Acmeda about the status and direction of the project. This experience was extremely beneficial, as it was not only the most extensive SolidWorks project that I had done at the time, but it also gave me insight into fabrication and manufacturing methods.

Projects

Barrel-Vault Canopy


This design consisted of almost thirty unique parts, and over one hundred total parts in the final assembly. Just before I left ARCADIA to return to school, a full size prototype had been fabricated. In order to get to this point, I had to work off our designer's AutoCAD orthographic drawings for each component, and help to design some of the components as well. Unfortunately, the product is still in production so I can not provide detailed pictures of CAD or go into specifics about the canopy's function at this time.

Throughout this project, I learned how important it is to not only think about the design and functionality of the product, but the fabrication of it as well. With each component, we had to think holistically about how it was going to interact with the other parts of the canopy, and how the entire unit was going to come together.

The photos above shows a detail from the umbrella design project. It was one of the coolest feelings to see a part I had been staring at on the computer for hours come to life and lie in my hands. The slot on the top of the joint holds four beams in place. There were four of these identical joints needed in the assembly of the umbrella, and four other unique joints were needed to complete the design.

Panel Stand Design


Another company came to Arcadia looking to design a stand to elegantly showcase their product. Unfortunately, I can not go into any detail about the description and function of this product as they are still in the middle of the design phase and remain confidential. However, I can say this stand includes stainless steel and poplar shims that hold the product in place. The stand is about five feet tall and contains 10 unique fixtures with a final assembly of 23 parts. The feet are able to rotate so the stand can lie flat on a single plane for transportation purposes. This project was very cool because I was given the freedom to ideate and sketch the initial ideas for what the design would look like. I explored hinged feet, flat circular static ones, and a variety of frame shapes and sizes. We ended up going with this design which allows the showcased product to slide into the stand at the top of the frame and are held in place by a stainless steel channel and poplar shim that mirrors the irregular shape of the product's edge. This is design is currently being fabricated and sent to the company for their meeting later this month.
