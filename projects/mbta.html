Project Information

During the summer of 2017, I took a graduate-level course at Northeastern focused on project management of technical projects in engineering disciplines. Our semester project was a culmination of what we had learned throughout the course represented by the formation of a project proposal. My team consisted of me and four other classmates. Together, we decided to write a proposal targeting the City of Boston and the Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority (MBTA) for the improvement of the MBTA’s Green Line.

Each section of the proposal was written as we learned about that part of the project in lecture. The course was organized into three parts:

  1. In Project Initiation we learned about the role of the project manager and strategic management techniques like negotiation and dealing with conflict

  2. In Project Planning we focused on the many aspects required to plan a future successful project. This included everything from scheduling, work breakdown structures, resource allocation and budgeting, and most importantly risk management. This section also included learning specific techniques and tools to help with these elements of a project’s plan including Gantt Charts, RACI and Stakeholders matrices, FMEA Risk Analysis and more.

  3. In Project Execution we discussed the crucial responsibilities of a Project Manager once a project is underway. This included project monitoring, control, auditing, and finally, project termination.

This course ended in the completion of a project proposal that aimed to improve the MBTA’s Green Line reliability and community approval. It seeks to do so through the implementation of new software and digital tools to improve user experience, while not dismantling the existing transportation infrastructure. The proposal, complete with a letter of transmittal addressed to the director of Business Development at the MBTA, is a representation of the skills and knowledge my teammates and I learned from this course. Personally, I was able to take the project management skills I learned working at Hasbro, and the insight I took from my experienced Project Managers at Continuum, into the classroom to learn how to be a project manager myself. I enjoyed this experience as it furthered my interest in pursuing project management in the technical and design fields in the future.
